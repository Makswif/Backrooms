The first picture of the Dullers

Do's and don'ts

Do:

  • Keep direct eye contact with Dullers once seen.

Don't:

  • Run away once you see one.
  • Move around the area.
  • Attempt to come in contact.

Discovery

One of the earliest recorded videos of the Backrooms shows a wanderer frantically fleeing from Dullers in a section of Level 0 marked with scratched writings. Despite this evidence, the origin of the wired areas where Dullers are commonly found remains a mystery. Without understanding the source of these areas, the true origin of the Dullers also remains unknown.

Description:

The Dullers are sinister, stick-like entities believed to be formed from ceiling wires in Level 0. They are extremely aggressive toward wanderers and other beings, often dwelling in dark, wire-filled areas of Level 0. Their appearance is eerily humanoid, though they possess unsettling, alien-like features that set them apart.

Little is known about the Dullers beyond their hostility toward anything that moves. They are dark gray and humanoid in form but exhibit two distinctly inhuman traits. Encounters with Dullers are extremely dangerous and can result in death or worse for the victim. These entities are not only incredibly fast but also relentless in pursuing their prey, though they can eventually tire.

However, they are blind and rely solely on detecting movement, making them essentially harmless if you remain still and silent in their presence.

Behaviors

Dullers primarily reside in dark, wired areas but have been known to occasionally emerge in search of prey. During their hunts, they leave behind cryptic messages scratched onto walls. These warnings, often reading "Don't move, stay still" or "Don't move!" serve as chilling signs of their presence, as reported by wanderers.

Despite their blindness, Dullers can detect movement through sound, making them extremely dangerous if approached carelessly. When nearby, they remain still and non-aggressive unless their victim moves, triggering a relentless pursuit. The fate of those caught by Dullers is unknown, but rumors suggest it is unspeakably horrific.

Interestingly, Dullers have been observed killing various other entities, such as Hounds, Death Moths, Skin-Stealers, Smilers, and even Partygoers. However, for reasons unknown, they do not attack other Dullers or Transporters.

Biology

Dullers are tall, gray entities with a humanoid appearance and a skeletal structure made of wires. Their most striking features include a mouth filled with long, sharp teeth at its center and elongated wired arms ending in sharp, claw-like appendages. While it is speculated that these claws are used to tear apart their prey, this remains unconfirmed.

Their stance is unsettlingly unnatural, and their walking motion mimics that of a human, adding to their eerie presence. Highly aggressive, Dullers are capable of running at incredible speeds. However, they are known to tire after prolonged chases, offering a slim chance of escape for their prey.
