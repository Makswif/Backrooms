Death Moths exhibit varying levels of hostility and biological traits depending on the level they inhabit. The Hive, regarded as their origin, is where they are the most dangerous and aggressive. However, individual Death Moths—whether residing in the same level or not—can display differing degrees of danger and hostility.

Appearance

Information regarding Death Moths is relatively lackluster due to the species' unpredictable nature. Commonly, Death Moths share a similar appearance to the Acherontia genus, which is a species native to the Frontrooms, more specifically in southern Europe and a vast majority of Africa, though they will occasionally migrate to the British Isles.

Don't:

  • Attempt to aggravate the moths or disturb the hives

Description:

Death Moths may differ between instances, but two key traits are consistently observed. Firstly, these moths exhibit a remarkable resemblance to those of the genus Acherontia. Secondly, they are unusually large, far exceeding the size of moths commonly found in the Backroooms, although smaller variants have occasionally been noted. Studies have established that Death Moths inhabit most levels of the Backrooms.

Little is known about the Dullers beyond their hostility toward anything that moves. They are dark gray and humanoid in form but exhibit two distinctly inhuman traits. Encounters with Dullers are extremely dangerous and can result in death or worse for the victim. These entities are not only incredibly fast but also relentless in pursuing their prey, though they can eventually tire.

Habitat

Death Moths are believed to originate from The Hive but are primarily found on Level 5 for reasons that remain unclear. Interestingly, smaller specimens are frequently reported in levels where Death Moths are not officially documented, suggesting their potential presence in additional areas.

Behaviors

The information above requires clarification, as all recorded specimens mentioned were male. Male Death Moths are notably smaller than their female counterparts. Additionally, Death Moths exhibit distinct behavioral patterns based on their gender, which can be summarized as follows:

Male

  • Skittish
  • Males, unlike females, can be tamed via Almond Water (not recommended)
  • Once tamed, they can be utilized as a means of defense.
  • When instructed to search for other wanderers, they may guide you to a Duller, Faceling, or Skin-Stealer.
  • When gathered in groups, male Death Moths exhibit a noticeably more mischievous nature.
  • Male Death Moths possess strong instincts when encountering a female specimen, often attempting to intrigue or entice her into becoming a mate.

Do's and Don'ts:

Do:

  • Avoid contact and do not act aggressive
