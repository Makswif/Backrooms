This image depicts two True Facelings, one male and one female.

Behaviours:

Facelings navigate the Backrooms much like humans, steering clear of hostile entities whenever possible and engaging with naturally occurring objects. They rarely interact with other entities, though some have been seen accompanied by a hound. Despite their lack of facial features, Facelings seem to possess vision.

Facelings exhibit no discernible personality traits and do not retaliate when provoked. Their typical response to external stimuli is to flinch or alter their walking direction. However, if faced with life-threatening harm, they may attempt to flee.

Description:

Facelings are a humanoid species that need little introduction. Frequently encountered across numerous levels, they are well known to anyone even vaguely familiar with the Backrooms.

These entities are primarily distinguished by their striking resemblance to humans, both in appearance and behavior. Their anatomy closely mirrors that of a human, though they seemingly do not require internal organs to survive or function. Facelings also imitate human activities, roles, and routines, often with only a superficial grasp of their true purpose.

Additionally, these creatures appear to follow human population trends. They are most commonly found in levels with urban environments or significant human presence such as Level 11. Any level featuring remnants of human architecture is likely to host a faceling population. Furthermore, their numbers seem to be linked to human population density. Though they have always been prevalent, the recent expansion of humanity across the Backrooms has seemingly led to a rise in faceling numbers as well.

Do's and Don'ts:

Do:

  • Treat Facelings with kindness and respect their personal space.
  • If you need help, try communicating with them most Facelings are willing to assist however they can.
  • Report any sightings of newly discovered Faceling variants.

Don't:

  • Do not taunt, provoke, or attack these entities.
  • Avoid appearing as a threat when encountering Facelings.
  • Refrain from teaching offensive words or terms to any Faceling.
