A sketch of a hound.

Biology:

A Hound resembles a human with long black hair growing from its head. They have sharp claws and an abnormally large mouth filled with sharp teeth, though this is often hidden by their thick hair. As previously mentioned, they move on all fours and possess great strength when attacking, despite their thin, frail appearance. Hounds hatch from eggs that come from Level 276.

Habitats

Hounds are most frequently encountered on Levels 1, 2, and 3, similar to skin-stealers. They can also be found on many other quantum-stable positive levels. However, for reasons that remain widely debated, they are rarely seen on levels above Level 12.

Don't:

  • If you encounter a Hound, it's crucial to run away immediately. Avoid any actions that might make you appear weak or vulnerable, as this could provoke an aggressive response.
  • Attempting to tame or befriend Hounds is highly dangerous and not recommended. They are hostile creatures by nature and can become aggressive without warning. It’s best to avoid any interaction with them and prioritize your safety.
  • Touching Hounds without proper protective equipment is extremely dangerous and should be avoided at all costs. Their sharp claws and teeth, along with their aggressive nature, make any direct contact highly risky. Always ensure you are adequately protected if you must interact with them, but it's best to avoid any contact altogether.

Description:

Hounds are humanoid creatures with canine features. They are characterized by disheveled black hair, large mouths full of sharp teeth, long bony limbs with pointed claws, and completely white eyes devoid of irises or pupils. Moving on all fours like dogs, they earn their name from this resemblance. These entities are among the more frequently encountered hostile beings in many levels of the Backrooms, though they appear less often in the deeper levels. Highly dangerous, Hounds become aggressive upon spotting someone in a hostile state but can be deterred under certain circumstances (see Behaviors). It is strongly advised to avoid Hounds at all times, even when they appear neutral.

Habitat

Hounds turn aggressive as soon as they spot a human. However, they can be momentarily deterred and stalled if you make direct eye contact with them. If you hear a Hound growling, it's best to remain out of sight and keep your distance until the danger passes.

Do's and Don'ts:

Do:

  • If a Hound approaches you, make sure to stare it down first to momentarily intimidate it, then quickly run away to put distance between you and the creature.
  • It's best to actively avoid Hounds whenever possible. Stay out of their line of sight and avoid areas where they are likely to appear to reduce the risk of encountering them.
