Almond water is a liquid found in various locations throughout The Backrooms, commonly in small bottles. Despite being extremely common, it is very valuable due to its regenerative properties and nutritional value.

Bottles of Almond Water found in Level 0.

As noted earlier, there is a possibility that Almond Water may be tampered with by other groups, individuals, or entities. To ensure safety, the general rule is: "When in doubt, boil the water." If resources allow, it is advisable to boil Almond Water as a precaution before drinking, even if there are no immediate signs of tampering. This step can be skipped only in emergency situations where boiling is not feasible.

Almond Water is widely used by wanderers as a convenient and efficient source of nutrition. Consuming one full bottle provides the equivalent of a healthy, 600-calorie meal, making three bottles per day sufficient to meet the nutritional needs of an average, healthy individual. Despite being the first object discovered by The M.E.G., the mechanism behind its nutritional value remains a mystery. Its abundance across most levels makes Almond Water an accessible way to sustain physical health without relying on traditional food sources. This eliminates the need for husbandry and enhances efficiency in more stable environments like Level 11. In addition to calories, Almond Water supplies essential nutrients, including protein, sodium, and fat, further contributing to its value as a dietary staple.

Description:

Object 1, better known as "Almond Water," is a naturally flavored drink widely available across various levels of the Backrooms. It serves as the primary source of hydration and is essential for preventing severe dehydration during travel. The beverage has a distinct sweet flavor, combining almond and vanilla notes with hints of purified rose water. Almond Water is typically stored in different containers, such as plastic bottles, metal cans, glass wine bottles, or cartons. It can also be dispensed from water fountains or found in concentrated forms within bodies of water. The reasons behind its abundance in the Backrooms, as well as its origins, remain unknown.

The exact process by which Almond Water manifests within the levels of the Backrooms remains a mystery. While it can be found, no instances of its appearance have ever been directly observed or documented. Current understanding suggests that Almond Water manifests in areas where no one can witness the event. This could occur in an unoccupied room or behind objects in spaces already occupied by wanderers. There appears to be no discernible pattern or logic governing where Almond Water will appear, as its manifestations seem entirely random.

Consuming Almond Water has been noted to alleviate minor symptoms of illness and improve overall well-being. It is also capable of neutralizing the effects caused by hostile entities. Wanderers frequently report feeling a heightened sense of focus after drinking it. While Almond Water offers a modest amount of nutritional value, it remains unclear whether its effects stem purely from its nutritional properties or from something more anomalous. In rare cases, unique types of Almond Water may produce additional, less common effects.

Over time, Almond Water will expire if not properly preserved. Once expired, mold begins to grow in the liquid, typically appearing in shades of white, blue, or green. Expired Almond Water is described by wanderers as having a foul odor resembling rotten milk or cheese. Consuming expired Almond Water is strongly discouraged, as it can be toxic, with effects comparable to those of ingesting Cashew Water. Symptoms of poisoning include, but are not limited to, weakness, fatigue, dementia, insomnia, internal bleeding, and aneurysms. In severe cases, consuming Almond Water that has been expired for extended periods has resulted in the deaths of wanderers.

The contents of Almond Water bottles may vary slightly in taste, but the liquid consistently resembles water in texture and appearance. As long as it has not been tampered with, Almond Water is generally safe to drink. However, certain levels can impose an anomalous effect that causes symptoms similar to cyanide poisoning in those who consume it. There is no predictable pattern for which levels produce this effect, and no way to determine it in advance—though any such warnings will be noted on the corresponding level’s page. This harmful effect can be neutralized by boiling the Almond Water. Adding a small pinch of Firesalt is an effective method to instantly boil the liquid and kill any bacteria, though excessive use of Firesalt may leave the water with a slightly spicy taste.
